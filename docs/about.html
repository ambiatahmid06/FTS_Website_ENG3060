Our Founder

Holice P. Wood

Holice P. Wood has loved fishing since childhood, creating lasting memories on Lake Erie with his brothers. Those early mornings on the shoreline, before boats were an option, before technology made it easy, shaped who he is and what FTS Fishing would become.

He founded FTS Fishing to share that passion and build a community of shore fishing enthusiasts who had been left behind by a digital fishing world built for boat anglers.

Frustrated by shoreline anglers being overlooked in the digital age, Holice created FTS to bring technology, fair play, and real community to the water's edge. Today, FTS connects and empowers bank anglers everywhere, turning every cast into part of something bigger.

Our Story

The FTS Fishing Story

FTS was founded on the belief that every fishing experience is unique, whether it's about solitude, competition, or connection.

Frustrated by shoreline anglers being left behind in the digital age, we created FTS to bring technology, fair play, and real community to the water's edge. Today, FTS connects and empowers bank anglers everywhere, turning every cast into part of something bigger.

We are working collaboratively with Wayne State University faculty and students to push the boundaries of fishing technology, building platform innovations that will define the future of shore angling.

Core Values

What We Stand For

Empower Shoreline Anglers

Celebrate and organize a community of bank fishers, whether you fish solo or as part of a group. Shore anglers deserve the same tools and recognition as any tournament pro.

Champion Innovation

Deliver cutting-edge AR tools to make every outing smarter, more exciting, and more rewarding.

Promote Fair Competition

Launch inclusive shoreline fishing leagues for amateur, semi-pro, and pro anglers, where every cast and catch counts toward personal records or team victories.

Foster Real Community

Build a welcoming space for anglers to share stories, swap tips, and support conservation, alone or side by side with others who fish from the shore.
