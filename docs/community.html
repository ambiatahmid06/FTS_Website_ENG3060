Michigan DNR Stocks 18.9 Million Fish

Great news for shore anglers in Michigan! The Michigan DNR recently announced it stocked 18.9 million fish across the state's lakes and rivers this year — with thousands making their way into prime shore fishing spots. This represents one of the largest stocking programs in recent history. For bank anglers this means improved access to a wider variety of species including Walleye, Steelhead, Lake Trout, Salmon and Brown Trout.

Tips & Techniques 2025

Finding Fish from the Shore: 5 Pro Tips

One of the most effective strategies for shore anglers is identifying structural features visible from the bank. Look for points where you can see a transition between deep and shallow water — or identify where a current break might channel baitfish. Learn to read the water and let the fish find you. Patience, positioning, and the right time of day make all the difference.

Tips & Techniques 2025

Curl Tail Grub — A Shore Angler's Best Friend

The curl tail grub is one of the most versatile and effective lures for shore fishing. Whether you're targeting bass, walleye, or panfish — this simple bait can be rigged in many ways to cover all depths. The best setup is a 1/8 or 1/4 oz jig head with a 3-4 inch curl tail grub in chartreuse, white, or motor oil color. Work it slowly along the bottom to mimic a crawfish or injured baitfish.

Knots 2025

Palomar Knot — Strongest All-Purpose Knot

The Palomar knot is one of the strongest and most reliable fishing knots. It retains nearly 100% line strength and is simple to tie — making it the go-to for attaching hooks, swivels, and lures. Thread 4-6 inches of line through the eye of the hook, double it back through again, then tie a loose overhand knot, pass the hook through the loop, and pull tight. Simple, fast, and nearly unbreakable.

Knots 2025

Improved Clinch Knot — The Classic Standard

The Improved Clinch Knot is one of the most widely used fishing knots in the world. It's fast to tie, dependable under pressure, and works with mono, fluoro, or braid. Pass 6 inches of line through the hook eye, make 5-7 wraps around the main line, thread the tag end through the loop near the eye, then pass it back through the large loop you just created. Wet the knot and pull tight. Clip the tag end close.

Community Guidelines

Respect the Water

Always practice catch-and-release properly, follow local regulations, and leave no trace. We fish because we love it — protect it for future generations.

Respect Each Other

This is a welcoming community for all anglers, regardless of skill level or background. Treat fellow anglers with the respect you expect on the water.

Honest Reporting

All catch submissions and league scores must be verified and honest. The FTS community is built on trust — PORT technology helps keep it that way.
