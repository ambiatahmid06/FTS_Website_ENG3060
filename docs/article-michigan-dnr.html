18.9 Million Fish Stocked by Michigan DNR by WILX News 10

By FTS Fishing | News

This post is based in part on reporting from WILX News 10.

According to their recent coverage, the Michigan Department of Natural Resources (DNR) has stocked approximately 18.9 million fish across the state. These fish represent ten different species and have a combined weight of 331.5 tons.

"Fish stocking—the practice of releasing hatchery-raised fish into natural bodies of water—helps support high-quality fishing opportunities for both residents and visitors."

Fish stocking is a vital part of Michigan's conservation strategy. Not only does it support recreational angling—a major contributor to local economies—but it also helps restore native species, balance aquatic ecosystems, and improve biodiversity in both inland and Great Lakes waters.

For shore anglers across Michigan, this is great news. With thousands of freshly stocked fish making their way into prime bank fishing spots, species like Walleye, Steelhead, Lake Trout, Salmon, and Brown Trout are more accessible than ever this season.

This represents one of the largest stocking programs in recent history — a major win for Michigan's fishing community and a testament to the DNR's ongoing commitment to conservation and public access to quality fishing.
