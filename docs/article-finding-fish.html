Finding Fish from the Shore: 5 Pro Tips

By FTS Fishing | Fishing Tips

Finding fish is the one key to catching fish. Knowing where fish live will improve your odds of catching!

  • Fish prefer places where they can hide, both to forage, and to prevent becoming forage to bigger fish. Look for places that create shade in the water, such as laydown trees, stumps, brush piles and rock piles. Other good places are boat docks, and those with brush placed underneath are preferred. Fish will live in good numbers around these places, along with bridge piers.
  • Different species of fish prefer different water temperatures, but a good rule of thumb is to start shallow in the spring, and try deeper water as the summer warms the water.
  • On sunny days, fish will be closer to cover than they will on cloudy days.
  • Vary your approach, both size and colors. As a general rule, light colors work better on bright, sunny days, and darker colors work better on overcast days, or murky water conditions.
  • Fish can become accustomed to your lures, both in action and colors. When you have caught several in one area with one style of bait, before moving on, switch to a different type of bait, or a different color. Many times this will add extra fish to your creel!
  • Use the right line size for the lure you're using. Generally, line from 4 to 8lb test for crappie and panfish, 8 to 14 for bass. Always check your drag periodically during the day, to make sure you are ready for the fish of a lifetime!
